The Life of Peter



In the early months of 2012 as I was nearing the end of my business degree, I came across something that changed my life forever. It was 2am in the morning as I sat in my room, reading on articles on how responsive web comics will soon take over and old traditional style of comics will soon be a thing of the past. As a big fan of comics, I’ve always found it difficult to read comics books or books of any kind while using a tablet. The idea that something so radical will soon come and bring changes to how any type of information will be consumed by users simply fascinated me to no end. I also began to notice that certain things bug me more than it should, for example, a poorly constructed road that may look great in terms of design but does poorly in terms of directing traffic flow or messenger bags slides and get tied up at the straps causes movement restrictions for the users. It was then that I began to realize the importance of user experience design and the value of a well crafted product.

In the two years that follow, I began my transition from working in the digital marketing field to a graphic designer and now finally a UX Designer. From 2015 to the present, I had the privilege of working with companies such as BlackBerry, Microsoft, and Electronic Arts (EA) which allowed me to validate my knwoledge as a UX practitioner, which range from conducting user testing to creating rapid prototypes to communicate ideas to stakeholders, my method of practice revolves around user centered design principles that are backed by research based on the users’ needs as well as the business goals.
